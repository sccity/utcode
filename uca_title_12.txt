Utah Code Annotated § 12-1-11 Collection fee -- Convenience fees.
-----------------------------------------------------------------
Utah Code Annotated § 12-1-11(1): 13-38a-102 As used in this section:
Utah Code Annotated § 12-1-11(1)(a): "Creditor" is as defined in 15 U.S.C. Sec. 1692a.
Utah Code Annotated § 12-1-11(1)(b): "Debt" means an obligation or alleged obligation to pay money arising out of a transaction for money, property, insurance, or services.
Utah Code Annotated § 12-1-11(1)(c): "Debtor" means a person obligated or allegedly obligated to pay a debt.

Utah Code Annotated § 12-1-11(1)(e): "Third party debt collection agency" means:
Utah Code Annotated § 12-1-11(1)(e)(i): a debt collector as defined in 15 U.S.C. Sec. 1692a; or
Utah Code Annotated § 12-1-11(1)(e)(ii): a person who would be a debt collector under 15 U.S.C. Sec. 1692a, except that the person does not use an instrumentality of interstate commerce or the mail.

Utah Code Annotated § 12-1-11(2)(a): (2)(a)(iv) A creditor may require a debtor to pay a collection fee in addition to any other amount owed to the creditor for a debt if:

Utah Code Annotated § 12-1-11(2)(a)(ii): the creditor contracts with a third party debt collection agency or licensed attorney to collect the debt;
Utah Code Annotated § 12-1-11(2)(a)(iii): the third party debt collection agency with which the creditor contracts is registered under this title;
Utah Code Annotated § 12-1-11(2)(a)(iv): there is a written agreement between the creditor and the debtor that:

Utah Code Annotated § 12-1-11(2)(b)(ii): 40% of the principal amount owed to the creditor for a debt.
