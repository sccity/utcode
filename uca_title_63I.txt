Utah Code Annotated § 63I-1-101 Title.
--------------------------------------


Utah Code Annotated § 63I-1-102 Agency defined -- Periodic termination of statutes and agencies -- Legislative review.
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Utah Code Annotated § 63I-1-102(1): As used in this chapter, "agency" means any state authority, board, commission, department, division, office, or other agency, and the statute that established it.
Utah Code Annotated § 63I-1-102(2): The Legislature finds that the state should not regulate any area unless the regulation is necessary to protect the health, safety, and welfare of the public.
Utah Code Annotated § 63I-1-102(3): Part 2, Repeal Dates Requiring Committee Review by Title In order to make state government more productive and responsive to the people, it is necessary to place many of the statutes and agencies of state government under
Utah Code Annotated § 63I-1-102(4): It is the purpose of this chapter to terminate any statute or agency that is not meeting a clear public purpose, and to improve the ability of state government to meet and fulfill legitimate public purposes.


Utah Code Annotated § 63I-1-103 Guidelines for conduct of review.
-----------------------------------------------------------------
Utah Code Annotated § 63I-1-103(1): 
Utah Code Annotated § 63I-1-103(1)(a): Any statute or agency scheduled for termination may be reviewed by an interim committee at the direction of:
Utah Code Annotated § 63I-1-103(1)(a)(i): Legislative Management Committee;
Utah Code Annotated § 63I-1-103(1)(a)(ii): the chairs of an interim committee; or
Utah Code Annotated § 63I-1-103(1)(a)(iii): an interim committee as approved by motion and majority vote of its membership.
Utah Code Annotated § 63I-1-103(1)(b): The review shall begin not later than one year before scheduled termination and end before January 1 of the year in which termination is scheduled.
Utah Code Annotated § 63I-1-103(2): In determining whether to reauthorize the statute or agency, the agency overseeing the statute or agency scheduled for termination shall clearly identify for the interim committee the public purpose and interest for which each statute or agency was originally created and clearly identify whether that public purpose and interest is still relevant.
Utah Code Annotated § 63I-1-103(3): The interim committee shall then consider:
Utah Code Annotated § 63I-1-103(3)(a): the extent to which the statute or agency has operated in the public interest and any areas in which the statute or agency needs to improve its ability to operate in the public interest;
Utah Code Annotated § 63I-1-103(3)(b): the extent to which existing statutes interfere with or assist the legitimate functions of the statute or agency, and any other circumstances including budgetary, resource, and personnel matters that have a bearing on the capacity of the statute or agency to serve the public interest;
Utah Code Annotated § 63I-1-103(3)(c): the extent to which the public has been encouraged to participate in the adoption of the rules established in connection with the statute or agency;
Utah Code Annotated § 63I-1-103(3)(d): the extent to which the statute's provisions or agency's programs and services are duplicative of those offered by other statutes or state agencies;
Utah Code Annotated § 63I-1-103(3)(e): the extent to which the objectives of the statute or agency have been accomplished and their public benefit;
Utah Code Annotated § 63I-1-103(3)(f): the adverse effect on the public of termination of the statute or agency; and
Utah Code Annotated § 63I-1-103(3)(g): any other matter relevant to the review.
Utah Code Annotated § 63I-1-103(4): It is the responsibility of any agency scheduled for termination or any agency which has oversight responsibilities for a statute scheduled for termination to seek its reauthorization with the Legislature.


Utah Code Annotated § 63I-1-104 Terminated authority -- Winding up of affairs.
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Any agency terminated under this chapter may continue in existence, if necessary to wind up its affairs, until July 1 of the year next succeeding the year of termination.

Utah Code Annotated § 63I-1-105 Reviewing committee -- Report of recommendations.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The reviewing committee shall submit a report of its recommendations, including proposed legislation and recommendations concerning the statute or agency, to the Legislature before January 1 of the year in which the agency is scheduled for termination.

Utah Code Annotated § 63I-1-106 Legislative reauthorization of statute or agency.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------
If the Legislature determines that the public interest requires the continued existence of the statute or agency, it shall reauthorize the existence of the statute or agency in this chapter.
