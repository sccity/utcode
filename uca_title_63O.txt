Utah Code Annotated § 63O-1-101 Definitions.
--------------------------------------------
As used in this title:Utah Code Annotated § 63O-1-101(1): "Architectural integrity" means the architectural elements, materials, color, and quality of the original building construction.
Utah Code Annotated § 63O-1-101(2): "Area of joint control" means all areas that are specified under this chapter as being under the direction and control of both the Legislature and the governor.
Utah Code Annotated § 63O-1-101(3): 63C-9-201 "Board" means the State Capitol Preservation Board created in Section
Utah Code Annotated § 63O-1-101(4): "Capitol hill" means the following, in Salt Lake City:
Utah Code Annotated § 63O-1-101(4)(a): the grounds, monuments, parking areas, buildings, structures, and other man-made and natural objects within the area bounded by 300 North Street, Columbus Street, 500 North Street, and East Capitol Boulevard;
Utah Code Annotated § 63O-1-101(4)(b): the White Community Memorial Chapel, including the grounds, monuments, parking areas, buildings, structures, and other man-made and natural objects on the property;
Utah Code Annotated § 63O-1-101(4)(c): the Council Hall Travel Information Center, including the grounds, monuments, parking areas, buildings, structures, and other man-made and natural objects on the property;
Utah Code Annotated § 63O-1-101(4)(d): the Daughters of the Utah Pioneers Building and the Carriage House, including:
Utah Code Annotated § 63O-1-101(4)(d)(i): the grounds, monuments, parking areas, buildings, structures, and other man-made and natural objects on the property; and
Utah Code Annotated § 63O-1-101(4)(d)(ii): the other state-owned property within the area bounded by Columbus Street, North Main Street, and Apricot Avenue;
Utah Code Annotated § 63O-1-101(4)(e): the Central Plant, located to the southeast of the intersection of 500 North and Columbus Street;
Utah Code Annotated § 63O-1-101(4)(f): the state-owned property within the area bounded by Columbus Street, Wall Street, and 400 North Street; and
Utah Code Annotated § 63O-1-101(4)(g): the state-owned property within the area bounded by Columbus Street, West Capitol Street, and 500 North Street.
Utah Code Annotated § 63O-1-101(5): "Governor's area" means all areas, other than an area of joint control, that are specified under this chapter as being under the direction and control of the governor.
Utah Code Annotated § 63O-1-101(6): "House Building" means the west building on capitol hill that is located northwest of the State Capitol, southwest of the North Building, and west of the Senate Building.
Utah Code Annotated § 63O-1-101(7): "Legislative area" means all areas, other than an area of joint control, that are specified under this chapter as being under the direction and control of the Legislature.
Utah Code Annotated § 63O-1-101(8): "Legislative day" means:
Utah Code Annotated § 63O-1-101(8)(a): a day during the annual general session of the Legislature;
Utah Code Annotated § 63O-1-101(8)(b): a day during a special session of the Legislature;
Utah Code Annotated § 63O-1-101(8)(c): a day during which the House of Representatives is convened under Utah Constitution, Article VI, Section 17;
Utah Code Annotated § 63O-1-101(8)(d): a day during which the Senate is convened under Utah Constitution, Article VI, Section 18;
Utah Code Annotated § 63O-1-101(8)(e): a day during a veto override session; or
Utah Code Annotated § 63O-1-101(8)(f): a day designated by the Legislative Management Committee as a legislative day for meetings of the House of Representatives, the Senate, or a committee, task force, caucus, or other group of the legislative branch.
Utah Code Annotated § 63O-1-101(9): "North Building" means the building on capitol hill that is located north of the State Capitol, northeast of the House Building, and northwest of the Senate Building.
Utah Code Annotated § 63O-1-101(10): "Senate Building" means the building on capitol hill that is located northeast of the State Capitol, southeast of the North Building, and east of the House Building.
Utah Code Annotated § 63O-1-101(11): "State Capitol" means the building dedicated as the Utah State Capitol in 1916.
Utah Code Annotated § 63O-1-101(12): 
Utah Code Annotated § 63O-1-101(12)(a): "Tunnels" means all utility and security tunnels, corridors, and hallways on the basement level of capitol hill.
Utah Code Annotated § 63O-1-101(12)(b): "Tunnels" does not include the underground parking.

Utah Code Annotated § 63O-2-201 State Capitol Preservation Board -- Creation -- Membership.
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Utah Code Annotated § 63O-2-201(1): There is created the State Capitol Preservation Board.
Utah Code Annotated § 63O-2-201(2): The board shall consist of the following 11 members:
Utah Code Annotated § 63O-2-201(2)(a): the governor, or the lieutenant governor acting as the governor's designee;
Utah Code Annotated § 63O-2-201(2)(b): the president of the Senate or the president's designee, who shall be a member of the Senate;
Utah Code Annotated § 63O-2-201(2)(c): the speaker of the House of Representatives or the speaker's designee, who shall be a member of the House of Representatives;
Utah Code Annotated § 63O-2-201(2)(d): the state treasurer;
Utah Code Annotated § 63O-2-201(2)(e): the state attorney general;
Utah Code Annotated § 63O-2-201(2)(f): two members of the Senate appointed by the president of the Senate, one from the majority party and one from the minority party;
Utah Code Annotated § 63O-2-201(2)(g): two members of the House of Representatives appointed by the speaker of the House of Representatives, one from the majority party and one from the minority party;
Utah Code Annotated § 63O-2-201(2)(h): the chief justice of the Supreme Court or the chief justice's designee, who shall be a member of the Supreme Court; and
Utah Code Annotated § 63O-2-201(2)(i): the state historic preservation officer.

Utah Code Annotated § 63O-2-601 Responsibility for items.
---------------------------------------------------------
Furniture, furnishings, fixtures, works of art, and decorative objects for which the board has responsibility under this chapter are not subject to the custody or control of the State Library Board, the State Library Division, the Division of Archives and Records Service, the Utah Historical Society, the Division of Arts and Museums, the arts collection committee of the State of Utah Alice Merrill Horne Art Collection, or any other state agency.

Utah Code Annotated § 63O-2-602 Transfer of certain historical items.
---------------------------------------------------------------------
Utah Code Annotated § 63O-2-602(1): 
Utah Code Annotated § 63O-2-602(1)(a): A state agency or other state entity that possesses a state-owned item identified by the executive director and the board as an item of historical significance that was at one time located in the capitol hill facilities shall transfer the item to the inventory of the board at the direction of the executive director not later than the 60th day after the date that the executive director notifies the agency or entity.
Utah Code Annotated § 63O-2-602(1)(b): The state agency or other state entity shall subsequently transfer physical possession of the item to the board in accordance with policies and procedures established by the board.
Utah Code Annotated § 63O-2-602(2): This section does not apply to records or documents in the custody of the Division of Archives and Records Service.
